You've heard both terms thrown around. Sports massage. Deep tissue. Maybe you've been told you need one or the other, but nobody actually explained the difference.

Let's fix that.

## What Is Sports Massage?

Sports massage targets the specific muscles you use most in training or competition. It's not a relaxation treatment. It's focused, deliberate work designed to improve your range of motion, reduce recovery time, and prevent injuries before they happen.

If you're training for something — a half marathon, a powerlifting meet, a Sunday league season — sports massage works around your schedule and your sport.

At [HH Massage Therapy](/treatments), every sports massage session starts with understanding what you do, how often you train, and where your body is taking the hit.

## What Is Deep Tissue Massage?

Deep tissue massage goes after the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. It's slower, more intense, and designed to break down adhesions (those tight knots that won't shift on their own).

This isn't just for athletes. If you're sat at a desk all day and your shoulders feel like concrete, deep tissue work can release tension that's been building for months.

## So Which One Should You Book?

Here's the honest answer: it depends on what's going on with your body.

**Book sports massage if:**
- You train regularly and want to recover faster
- You've got a specific event coming up
- You want to maintain performance and prevent injury

**Book deep tissue if:**
- You've got chronic tension or pain that won't shift
- You're dealing with postural issues from desk work
- You want targeted relief in specific problem areas
